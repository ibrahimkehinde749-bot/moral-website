Grant Program Highlights: Administration for Children & Families (ACF); The Administration for Children and Families (ACF) promotes the economic and social well-being of families, children, individuals, and communities. ACF programs are designed to empower families and individuals to increase their independence and productivity; foster strong, healthy, and supportive communities that enhance quality of life and promote positive child development; and build partnerships with frontline service providers, states, local governments, and tribal communities to improve access to services through effective planning, reform, and integration. In addition, ACF addresses the unique needs, strengths, and capabilities of vulnerable populations, including individuals with developmental disabilities, refugees, and migrants.

Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ) The mission of the Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ) is to generate evidence that improves the safety, quality, accessibility, equity, and affordability of health care. AHRQ works within the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and collaborates with various partners to ensure that this evidence is effectively communicated and applied in practice. Through its grant programs, AHRQ supports research aimed at enhancing the quality, effectiveness, accessibility, and cost-efficiency of health care services.

National Institutes of Health (NIH) The National Institutes of Health (NIH) provides financial support through grants, cooperative agreements, and contracts to advance its mission of enhancing health, extending healthy lives, and reducing the burdens of illness and disability. While many NIH grants focus on research, the agency also offers funding opportunities in areas such as fellowships and training, career development, scientific conferences, resource development, and facility construction.
